May 2026 Essay 8 min read

This is a reusable single-post layout for future writing. Replace the title, metadata, summary, and body content with actual article material later.

The purpose of this template is to establish page rhythm rather than final content. A single article page should read more like a considered publication than a generic blog post screen.

That means the layout favors a narrow text column, consistent spacing, quiet metadata, and clear hierarchy between title, introduction, section headings, and body paragraphs.

Why keep a dedicated article template

Separating the archive list from the article page makes it easier to maintain a strong reading experience. The archive can scan quickly, while the article page can slow down and give the text room to breathe.

It also keeps future migration options open. If the site later adopts a generator such as Hexo, this page can still act as the visual reference for the post layout.

What to replace later

  • Post title
  • Date, category, and reading time
  • Summary paragraph
  • Body text, headings, images, and references

For now, this page exists only as a structural scaffold. It completes the route flow from homepage to archive to article without forcing you to write real content before you are ready.
